Getting Lost in a Familiar Place

Reclaiming our public space


The streets can be a liminal space. A place between other places. You go on them to get somewhere else - home, work, or a store. Usually we are only present on them in between spaces where we get or spend money.

Enter a Dérive. Take an unplanned journey through the urban landscape. Treat the street as it's own destination. Go on an walk, without needing a place to get to. Let yourself drift and follow your feelings.

Techniques

Go on a walk following one or several of these rules. Treat the street as it's own location which can, and should be, explored.

  • Follow a red vehicle.
  • Step on cracks in the pavement.
  • Pick a color. Go towards it.
  • Pick a building in the distance. Go towards it.
  • Go east for three blocks.
  • Flip a coin. Heads means turn left, tails means turn right.
  • Walk without a direction.
  • Look for rhyming signs.
  • Turn left or right. It's a free world.
  • Turn whenever you reach a red light.
  • When a street name starts with a vowel turn left.
  • Find an interesting tree.
  • Look at the chimneys.
  • Find a fancy car. Go in the direction it's wheels are pointing.
  • Find shade. Observe it.
  • Find a place to sit for five minutes. Watch.
  • At the next opportunity turn right.
  • Stop and listen. Follow that sound.
  • Pick any direction and go straight for five blocks.
  • Go towards laughter.
  • Turn left at the next corner.
  • Wave and smile at the next surveillance camera.
  • Head in the noisiest direction.
  • What is this area know for? Find the opposite.
